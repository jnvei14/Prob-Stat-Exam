Probability

Definition: Probability is the measure of how likely an event is to occur, ranging from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain). It helps quantify uncertainty and is widely used in decision-making, games, and predictions. Probability is calculated as the ratio of favorable outcomes to the total possible outcomes.

  • 0 means the event is impossible.
  • 1 means the event is certain.
    • Formula: Probability¬†(P) = Favorable Outcomes / Total Possible Outcomes

    Example: If you roll a standard 6-sided die, the probability of rolling a 4 is: P (rolling a 4) = 1/6 (There is 1 favorable outcome (rolling a 4), and 6 possible outcomes (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6)).

    Applications:

  • Weather forecasting (e.g., "There is a 70% chance of rain today.")
  • Risk analysis in finance or insurance.
  • Decision-making in games and sports.

It considers all possible ways items can be organized, such as arranging letters, forming unique passwords, or assigning positions. The number of permutations increases as more items are included in the calculation. Mathematically, it uses factorials to determine arrangements. Permutations are used in scheduling, seating arrangements, and ranking tasks where order is critical.
